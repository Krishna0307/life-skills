## Question 1
## What are the steps/strategies to do Active Listening?

* Stop distractions and focus on the speaker.
* Shows interest and respect.
* Let the speaker finish before responding.
* Use small responses like yes or no to show attention.
* Clarify doubts to understand better.
* Pay attention to tone, expressions, and gestures.

# Question 2

## According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening?

* Listen carefully to what the person is saying.
* Try to understand facts in the message.
* Repeat what you heard in your own words.
* Check with the speaker to confirm understanding.
* Do not interrupt or give quick advice.

# Reflection

## Question 3
## What are the obstacles in your listening process?

* Getting distracted by phone or surroundings.
* Thinking about my reply instead of listening fully.
* Interrupting the speaker while they are talking.
* Losing focus during long  conversations.
* Reacting emotionally (agreeing or disagreeing too quickly).

## Question 4
## What can you do to improve your listening?

* Focus on during conversations.
* Keep your phone away while someone is talking.
* Take notes if needed.
* Let the speaker finish before you respond.
* Ask questions to clear your doubts.

## Question 5
## When Do I Use Passive Communication?
* When I feel nervous in a new group
* When I want to avoid conflict.
* When I talk to seniors  figures.
* When I don’t want to hurt someone’s feelings.

## Question 6
## When I Use Aggressive Communication
* When I feel frustrated or stressed
* When I strongly disagree with someone
* When I feel something is unfair

## Question 7
## When I Use Passive-Aggressive Communication
* When I am upset but don’t say it directly
* Using sarcasm or indirect comments
* Ignoring someone (silent treatment)
* When I ignore someone instead of talking to them.

## Question 8
## How I Can Make My Communication Assertive
* Speak clearly and confidently without being rude
* Express my thoughts honestly .
* Listen to others before responding
* Maintain calm body language and tone
* Set boundaries politely
* Be open to feedback and discussion
